HOW in a question - writing techniques

PEWE

PEWE

P: Point - What is the point that the text is trying to make?
E: Evidence - Quote(s) from the text showing the point being made
W: Writer - Techniques that the writer is using
E: Explain - Explain how the evidence makes the point

TESCO

TESCO

T: Technique
E: Evidence
S: Statement
C: Connotations
O: On the reader

DAFORREST

DAFORREST

Techniques for persuasive writing

D: Direct address - You can do this by…
A: Alliteration - Big brown bear
F: Facts - The sky is blue, This is because…
O: Opinions - The sky is very beautiful
R: Rhetorical questions - Did you really think that would work?
R: Repetition - Why. Why, WHY!!
E: Emotive language - The helpless children, pleaded desperately for a crumb of bread
S: Statistics - 80% of children think school is overrated
T: Triplets - See it, say it, sorted.

Techniques

TechniquesWhat they are used forExample
Metaphor, Simile, PersonificationBrings qualities of the image to the thing being described’Blue as the ocean’ this blue is vast and deep, maybe a bit dangerous or mysterious. ‘Blue as a rain drop’ this implies sadness.
RepetitionFocuses the reader’s attention on that thing. Sometimes for exaggeration.
Alliteration (repeated first sound), Sibilance (repeated /s/ sound)Focuses the reader’s attention on that thing. Sibilance: implies soft or sinister atmosphere.
Contrast: Oxymoron, juxtapositionHelps to express complicated human feelings and emotions. Sometimes it is used for humour.”Troubled pleasure”
ExaggerationBuilds emotions engaging the reader, building tensionTravelled back 100s of years
Emotive languageBrings the reader into the text so they experience the range of emotions with the speaker/character
Sensory languageBrings the reader into the text, it’s very engaging making the reader feel as if they are there.
TensionBringing the reader into the text, heightening their emotions. Making them ask questions and want an answer.”Don’t talk to wolves and don’t stray off the path.” we know she will, but why does she do it!?
